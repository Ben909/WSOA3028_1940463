How the digital divide affects us

The digital divide came into fruition early in the 1990â€™s when it became apparent that not every individual possesses the capability to operate computer systems or has access to the internet. This led to an economic and social inequality that impacts the spread of information and communication technologies. For example, the divide within countries such as the United States may refer to the inequalities between households, individuals, businesses or geographic areas usually at different socio-economic levels or other demographic categories. This also suggests that the digital divide phenomenon is linked to new forms of technology that develops in a global sphere. The divide between different countries or regions of the world is referred to as the global digital divide. This defines the technological gap in terms of access and usage to information between developing and developed countries on an internal scale. Additional factors such as income, education, geographic location, age and motivation further influences this divide.
