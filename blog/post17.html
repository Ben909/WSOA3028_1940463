Robots in Digital Spaces

In the digital world robots or ‘bots’ are scripted software programs that are designed to do repetitive automated tasks. Bots have been around since the beginning of the internet with a purpose to harvest and store information. They are equipped with sensors that detect certain kinds of information in a manner that far exceeds human capabilities. Online search engines use bots to Fulfil our needs and grant instant access to information that we would otherwise take much longer to find ourselves. These bots have come a long way and are being developed for multiple purposes. Applications like Siri for example, are digital assistants that learn and memorize human interactions to help facilitate our day to day lives.

There are also a number of malicious bot that cause major harm to websites and account users. Known to some extent as trojans, these bots endlessly searches through websites for traces of personal information like e-mail addresses that are then used for spam mail. Even clicking on certain online links can trigger bots to search and collect information stored on digital devices. The most common type of bot is a web crawler or a web spider. These bots can be collectively be programmed to be used as cyber weapons. Once coordinated to the same webpage, the unexpected request volume can overwhelm the webserver and cause it to crash, losing valuable information in the process. This is known as a ddos attack or distributed denial of service attack. Websites are specifically developed to prevent bots from gaining access to their servers. To safeguard their websites and account information, developers have created the captcha. It requires the user to fill out a random series of numbers, letters or images before allowing entry into the site. The sole purpose of this design is to keep bots out of the website so that no harm can be done to its users.

Since their initial design, bots have become smarter and easier to make as open source databases like GitHub grants access to coded software for any individual needs. Social bots have begun to appear more frequently on sites like Twitter, Facebook and Reddit which is a major cause for concern. This is because bots can already be programmed to learn from material they find on the web. Often times this means bots behave in ways that programmers did not anticipate or intend. Questions of who is responsible for this unpredictable behavior are therefore made more complicated. We must carefully consider the interactions bots may have in the near future.
