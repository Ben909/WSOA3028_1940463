Metadata

The world wide web contains an amalgamation of data and information that grants global access to internet users. Metadata is a way to identify some elements surrounding a piece of information, so that we have a better idea of what that information is. The vast online collection of information constantly undergoes changes to improve their functionality in terms of how we recognize and access source material. They require descriptive values to establish how information resources are managed online. An example of metadata can be seen in libraries where books contain specific details about the authors, publishers and dates. The fferent categories that books can fall into are prime examples of metadata.
