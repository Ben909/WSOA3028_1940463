Twitter post

Twitter user Gretchen McCulloch claims, “Every so often, you end up down a Wikipedia rabbit hole that reminds you how Wikipedia’s editorship being male create huge content gaps for certain topics, especially those more typically associated with woman.” This is a very interesting statement that I am sure not many people are aware of. Most people do know that Wikipedia is a software that hosts collaborative projects and creates an encyclopedia of almost every concept in existence. The word ‘Wiki’ is Hawaiian for ‘quick’ and corresponds to how easily information can be accessed by users globally. The legitimacy of source material found on Wikipedia is always in question since its information can be produced and published by anyone in the world. For this reason, Wikipedia is filled with gaps of uncertainty about the content that it presents. Especially when male writers contribute to subject matters that may be better examined from a female perspective. I believe McColloch’s tweet emphasizes that we must be conscious and warry of Wikipedia content and the way it conducts and provides research. To counter this, Wikipedia provides additional links to external websites and resources that can be used to further expand on related topics..
